Executive Summary

CryptoClans is a blockchain-based strategy game inspired by Clash of Clans, offering an innovative experience in the GameFi space. Players establish their tribes, upgrade soldiers and leaders as NFTs, and create a dynamic economic ecosystem using custom meme coins.

Platforms

  • Android
  • iOS
  • Web Browser

Target Market

  • Strategy gamers (16+)
  • Cryptocurrency enthusiasts
  • Web3 investors

Blockchain Network

Polygon

Game Narrative

Crystal Territories Conflict

Years after major Earth conflicts, survivors discovered strange blue crystals falling from the sky with incredible powers. These crystals, now called "POL," can generate energy, transform metals, and grant special abilities to their holders.

In the new lands rising from the ashes of old civilization, people formed independent tribes. Each tribe seeks to expand its territory by gathering resources, constructing buildings, and training powerful warriors to control more POL resources.

Ethan, an ambitious young man with a handful of loyal forces and a POL crystal inherited from his father, decided to establish his own tribe. Initially, his territory was just a few wooden huts and a small gold mine, but his vision extended much further.

He quickly realized that POL was not just an energy source but could establish an entirely new economy. Using ancient knowledge found in temple ruins, Ethan minted the first tribal meme coin - "EthanCoin" - which his tribe members could use for internal trade.

Ethan's tribe grew rapidly. His warriors won battles, his builders constructed magnificent buildings, and his scientists discovered new technologies. Each victory increased EthanCoin's value, and each new tribe member strengthened its economy.

On the eastern horizon, the powerful "Cryptorium" tribe led by the cunning Malika watched Ethan's tribe's success with envy. Malika, who considered herself a master strategist, had created her own meme coin called "MalikaCoin" and was now planning to dominate more POL resources.

On a moonlit night, messengers from Ethan's tribe reported that Malika's army had been spotted at the eastern borders. Ethan knew his great test had come. He summoned the tribal council and held the first tribal DAO vote to decide their war strategy.

The countdown to the great battle began. The fate of two tribes, two economies, and two meme coins hung in the balance. Could the collective power of a tribal DAO prevail against the centralized strategy of a cunning leader? This was the beginning of the CryptoClans story...

1. Game Economy

1.1 Economic Layers

The CryptoClans economy consists of four distinct layers:

A) Base Resources

Base resources include gold, wood, diamonds, and elixir used for building and upgrading structures, training soldiers, and advancing the tribe:

Gold:

  • Required for training soldiers and upgrading certain buildings
  • Production: Gold mines (10-150 units per hour depending on level)
  • Storage: Gold warehouses (capacity 10,000 to 10,000,000 units)

Wood:

  • Required for constructing basic and defensive buildings
  • Production: Sawmills (8-120 units per hour depending on level)
  • Storage: Resource warehouses (shared capacity with other resources)

Diamonds:

  • Valuable resource for expediting progress and purchasing special items
  • Production: Diamond workshop (1-10 units per day depending on level)
  • Acquisition: Missions, achievements, purchase with POL

Elixir:

  • Required for upgrading soldiers and heroes
  • Production: Collection from events, victory in battles
  • Storage: Elixir tanks (limited capacity)

B) POL (Polygon Token)

POL token is the main currency of the game ecosystem, connecting the game to the Polygon network:

Uses:

  • Purchase diamonds and special packages
  • Quick construction and upgrading of buildings
  • Creating tribal tokens (meme coins)
  • Purchasing rare NFTs in the marketplace

Ways to Earn POL:

  • Daily missions: 0.01 to 0.1 POL
  • Victory in battles: 0.2 to 0.5 POL
  • Weekly events: Up to 10 POL
  • Seasonal competitions: 50 to 100 POL for top performers
  • Direct purchase from exchanges

Daily Limits:

  • Daily free POL earning cap: 0.5 POL
  • Cap increase with tribe level upgrades (up to 2 POL)

C) Tribal Tokens (Meme Coins)

Each tribe can produce its exclusive token, with value determined by the tribe's strength, activity, and popularity:

Creating Tribal Token:

  • Prerequisite: Tribal Hall Level 5
  • Creation cost: 20 POL or equivalent
  • Initial supply: 100,000 tokens (divided as follows)
  • 40% for tribe founder
  • 30% for distribution among initial members
  • 20% for liquidity pool and transactions
  • 10% reserved for future events

Uses:

  • Purchase exclusive tribal items
  • Upgrade tribal defensive features
  • Pay members for activity participation
  • Trade with other tribes

Value Growth Mechanisms:

  • Victory in battles: Each victory increases base value by 0.5%
  • Recruiting new members: Every 10 new members increases value by 5%
  • Token burning: Tribe founder can burn tokens to increase value
  • Economic activity: Higher transaction volume increases demand

Listing on Decentralized Exchanges:

  • Successful tokens with minimum 1000 transactions and 5000 POL market value can be listed on decentralized exchanges
  • Initial liquidity pool created with POL pair

D) NFT Assets

NFTs include soldiers, leaders, special lands, and rare items with real market value:

NFT Types:

  • Soldiers: From common to legendary level (value 1 to 100+ POL)
  • Leaders: Kings and queens (value 10 to 500+ POL)
  • Special buildings: Unique designs with special functions
  • Decorative items: Changing the appearance of tribes and characters

NFT Marketplace:

  • Internal market: Buying and selling with POL or tribal tokens
  • External market: Connection to major NFT marketplaces
  • Transaction fees: 2.5% (1% to developers, 1% to game treasury, 0.5% burned)

1.2 Anti-Inflation Mechanisms

To prevent inflation and maintain economic value in the network, the following mechanisms are implemented:

Tribal Token Supply Limitation:

  • Limited initial supply (100,000 tokens)
  • Controlled supply increase mechanism (maximum 5% per month)
  • Requirement to lock 20% of tokens for 6 months

Burning System:

  • 0.5% of each NFT transaction is burned
  • 2% of tribe upgrade costs are burned
  • Periodic burning events to reduce supply

Anti-Dump Smart Contracts:

  • Daily selling limit for holders of more than 5% of tokens
  • Price reduction mechanism for large sales

Dynamic Validation System:

  • Continuous monitoring of suspicious transactions
  • Automatic limitation for unusual transactions
  • Bot detection algorithm and multiple accounts

Token Price Floor:

  • Guaranteed buyback of tribal tokens at 50% of initial price
  • Reserving 10% of game revenue to support the market during crashes

1.3 Token Value and Inflation Management (Supplementary)

Dynamic Supply and Demand Mechanism:

  • Smart system that adjusts token supply based on active player count
  • During peak activity times, supply is reduced by 5%
  • During low activity times, burning increases by 10%

Diminishing Reward System:

  • Instead of fixed rewards for activities, rewards gradually decrease
  • Formula: Initial reward × (0.995^number of days since launch)

Maximum Final Supply:

  • For each tribal token, maximum final supply is set at 10 million tokens

1.4 Relationship Between POL Ecosystem and Tribal Tokens (Supplementary)

Value Bridge:

  • 20% of each tribal token's value is backed by POL
  • This amount is locked in smart contracts

Direct Transactions:

  • Ability to trade directly between different tribal tokens
  • Automatic conversion mechanism through POL pool

Value Network:

  • Ability to form "Economic Alliance" contracts between tribes
  • These contracts connect the value of tribal tokens

2. Tribe System

2.1 Creating a Tribe

The process of creating a new tribe is as follows:

Prerequisites:

  • Reaching player level 5 (minimum 10 hours of gameplay)
  • Having at least 5 POL or equivalent
  • Completing basic training (10 main missions)

Creation Steps:

  • Selecting tribe name, symbol, and color
  • Choosing tribe specialization type (Warrior, Merchant, Scientist)
  • Paying foundation costs (5 POL + 10,000 gold + 5,000 wood)
  • Selecting geographical location on the world map

Initial Settings:

  • Determining membership conditions (open, approval required, invitation only)
  • Setting minimum level for joining the tribe
  • Setting internal rules and tribal policies
  • Determining members' share of war spoils (5% to 20%)

Initial Buildings:

  • Tribal Hall (Level 1)
  • Barracks (Level 1)
  • One gold mine (Level 1)
  • One sawmill (Level 1)
  • One resource warehouse (Level 1)

2.2 Member Recruitment and Management

The tribe membership and management system is as follows:

Member Recruitment Methods:

  • Tribe search system: Players can search for tribes based on power, specialization, and membership conditions
  • Direct invitation: Sending invitations to friends or other players
  • Advertising: Cost of 1 POL to display the tribe in special offers for 3 days
  • Recruitment events: Attracting new members by offering special rewards (tribal token, free NFT)

Membership Levels:

  • Newcomer: Limited access to tribal resources (first 7 days)
  • Official member: Access to all basic tribe features
  • Elder: Ability to invite new members and manage resources
  • Deputy: Ability to manage wars and memberships
  • Leader: Complete control over the tribe

Participation and Scoring System:

  • Daily participation score based on activities (0-100 points)
  • Weekly rewards based on participation score
  • Monthly member ranking system
  • Share of tribal token based on rank and participation

Membership Benefits:

  • Receiving resource help from other members
  • Participating in tribal wars and receiving spoils
  • Access to exclusive tribal store
  • Share of tribal token if created
  • Defensive protection by stronger members

Leaving Tribe System:

  • 24-hour waiting period after exit request
  • 48-hour ban from joining a new tribe
  • Keeping 50% of earned tribal tokens
  • Losing rank and special benefits of previous tribe

2.3 Tribe Advancement and Progression

The tribe leveling and upgrade system is as follows:

Tribe Levels (1-20):

  • Level 1-5: Initial stage (access to basic buildings)
  • Level 6-10: Development stage (ability to create tribal token)
  • Level 11-15: Advanced stage (access to special buildings)
  • Level 16-20: Professional stage (ability to form tribal alliances)

Tribe Level Upgrade Methods:

  • Increasing number of active members
  • Upgrading key buildings
  • Victory in battles and earning military score
  • Completing tribal challenges

Tribe Score:

  • Calculated based on total power of members
  • Impact of tribal building levels
  • Impact of number and quality of NFTs in the tribe
  • Impact of economic activity and transaction volume

Level Upgrade Benefits:

  • Increased member capacity (from 10 at level 1 to 50 at level 20)
  • Access to more advanced buildings
  • Increased daily POL earning cap
  • Special defensive and offensive capabilities

2.4 Governance and Voting System

Voting Matters

The tribal DAO voting system is activated for the following:

Economic Decisions:

  • Changing token burn rate (once every 2 weeks)
  • Setting transaction tax (once a month)
  • Allocating budget for events and tribal activities (weekly)
  • Decision to list token on decentralized exchanges

Member Management:

  • Selecting deputies and senior members (every 30 days)
  • Expelling problematic members (with proposal from leader or 3 senior members)
  • Changing membership conditions and minimum required level

War Strategy:

  • Declaring war against rival tribes
  • Joining or leaving an alliance
  • Defensive strategy during coordinated attacks

Tribe Development:

  • Prioritizing key building upgrades
  • Decision about technology research and development path
  • Investment in special tribal NFT development

Polygon Network Voting Mechanism

Voting in CryptoClans is conducted on the Polygon network as follows:

Proposal Creation:

  • Each proposal is registered as a transaction on the Polygon blockchain
  • Proposal creation cost: 0.01 POL (to prevent trivial proposals)
  • Proposal includes: title, description, voting duration, and execution parameters

Vote Weight System:

  • Each tribal token = 1 voting right
  • Tokens locked in staking system = 1.5x voting rights
  • Minimum tokens required for voting: 100 tribal tokens
  • Before voting, tokens are locked for voting duration + 24 hours

Blockchain Vote Registration:

  • Vote is registered as a transaction with optimized gas cost on Polygon network
  • Use of Layer 2 to reduce transaction costs
  • Batch Voting capability to reduce costs

Quorum and Results Execution:

  • Minimum participation required (quorum): 20% of total circulating tokens
  • Regular decisions: Simple majority (50% + 1)
  • Important decisions: 66% majority
  • Critical decisions: 75% majority

Automatic Result Execution:

  • After voting ends, a smart contract checks the result and automatically implements changes if approved
  • Transparent voting results system with complete details displayed in tribal dashboard
  • History of all votes recorded on Polygon blockchain and reviewable

Security Features:

  • Fake vote detection system
  • Protection against Sybil attacks by checking account history
  • Anti-dump system to prevent vote accumulation by whales
  • Emergency veto mechanism by tribe leader (with usage limitation)

2.5 Multi-level Governance Mechanism (Supplementary)

Local Governance:

  • Daily decisions by tribe members (minimum 100 tokens)
  • Everyday operational issues like accepting new members, daily resource allocation

Middle Governance:

  • Strategic decisions by tribal council (top 10 members)
  • War tactical decisions, long-term resource allocation

Macro Governance:

  • Fate-determining decisions by all token holders
  • Fundamental changes in tribal policies, alliances, leadership change

4. Character System

4.1 Soldier Types

Soldiers in CryptoClans are divided into five main categories:

A) Frontline Soldiers

Powerful soldiers for close combat:

Swordsman:

  • Strengths: High attack power, low cost, fast training speed
  • Weaknesses: Medium health, vulnerable to archers
  • Special skill: (Level +5) Group attack - damage to multiple targets
  • Training cost: 100 gold, time: 1 minute
  • Upgrade levels: 1-10 (each level +20% power)

Guardian:

  • Strengths: High health, high damage resistance
  • Weaknesses: Low movement speed, medium attack power
  • Special skill: (Level +5) Defensive shield - 30% damage reduction for surrounding units
  • Training cost: 250 gold, time: 2 minutes
  • Upgrade levels: 1-10 (each level +25% health)

Jumper:

  • Strengths: Ability to jump over walls, high movement speed
  • Weaknesses: Low health, vulnerable to traps
  • Special skill: (Level +5) Bomber jump - area damage after jumping
  • Training cost: 300 gold, time: 2 minutes
  • Upgrade levels: 1-10 (each level +15% speed and power)

B) Archers

Ranged attack units:

Bowman:

  • Strengths: Long-range attack, fast fire power
  • Weaknesses: Low health, weak in close combat
  • Special skill: (Level +5) Arrow storm - attacking multiple targets simultaneously
  • Training cost: 200 gold, time: 2 minutes
  • Upgrade levels: 1-10 (each level +20% range and power)

Gunner:

  • Strengths: High fire power, high accuracy
  • Weaknesses: Low firing speed, high cost
  • Special skill: (Level +5) Crushing shot - severe damage to a single target
  • Training cost: 400 gold, time: 3 minutes
  • Upgrade levels: 1-10 (each level +30% single target power)

Fire Mage:

  • Strengths: Area damage, burning effect
  • Weaknesses: Very low health, high cost
  • Special skill: (Level +5) Fire storm - continuous damage in a large area
  • Training cost: 600 gold, time: 4 minutes
  • Upgrade levels: 1-10 (each level +25% area damage)

C) Heavy Units

Most powerful soldiers for destroying buildings:

Giant:

  • Strengths: Very high health, excellent destruction power
  • Weaknesses: Very low speed, easy target for defense
  • Special skill: (Level +5) Ground strike - area damage to buildings
  • Training cost: 1,000 gold, time: 10 minutes
  • Upgrade levels: 1-10 (each level +30% health and power)

Dragon Rider:

  • Strengths: Air attack, high speed, area damage
  • Weaknesses: Vulnerable to archers, very high cost
  • Special skill: (Level +5) Fiery breath - severe area damage
  • Training cost: 2,000 gold, time: 15 minutes
  • Upgrade levels: 1-10 (each level +25% power and range)

Stone Golem:

  • Strengths: High resistance to defense, extraordinary health
  • Weaknesses: Very low speed, high cost
  • Special skill: (Level +5) Division - after destruction, turns into two smaller golems
  • Training cost: 1,500 gold, time: 12 minutes
  • Upgrade levels: 1-10 (each level +35% health)

D) Support Units

Soldiers that help the team:

Healer:

  • Strengths: Ability to heal other units, medium speed
  • Weaknesses: Zero attack power, low health
  • Special skill: (Level +5) Group healing - healing multiple units simultaneously
  • Training cost: 500 gold, time: 5 minutes
  • Upgrade levels: 1-10 (each level +20% healing power)

Speed Mage:

  • Strengths: Increasing speed of other units, power buffer
  • Weaknesses: Low attack power, medium health
  • Special skill: (Level +5) Madness spell - 50% increase in attack speed of surrounding units
  • Training cost: 700 gold, time: 6 minutes
  • Upgrade levels: 1-10 (each level +15% effect on units)

Ghost:

  • Strengths: Invisibility, passing through walls
  • Weaknesses: Very low health, low attack power
  • Special skill: (Level +5) Sabotage - temporary disabling of enemy defense
  • Training cost: 800 gold, time: 8 minutes
  • Upgrade levels: 1-10 (each level +25% invisibility time)

E) NFT Soldiers

Unique soldiers with special capabilities and real value:

NFT Soldier Features:

  • Rarity: Common (80%), Rare (15%), Legendary (5%)
  • Special genes: Each NFT has 5 unique features
  • Fusion capability: Ability to merge two NFTs to create a stronger soldier
  • Higher upgrade level: Ability to upgrade to level 15 (versus level 10 for regular soldiers)

NFT Soldier Types:

  • Sniper: Elite archer with unparalleled accuracy and range

Base Price of NFT Soldiers:

Battle Phases

  • Battle outcome is determined based on force power, their arrangement, and bonuses from the kings' phase

  • Additional reward system for members who contribute the most to the treasury

War Treasury Benefits

5.2 Battle Rewards

Attack Reward Formula

A) Looting Attack

  • Base Resource Spoil: (20% of resources stored in opponent's tribe) × (destruction percentage ÷ 100)
  • Level Coefficient: 1 + (opponent tribe level - attacker tribe level) × 0.05 (minimum 0.8, maximum 1.5)
  • History Coefficient: 1 - (number of attacks on this tribe in past 24 hours × 0.1) (minimum 0.5)
  • Final Spoil: Base resource spoil × level coefficient × history coefficient

Additional Rewards:

  • In case of achieving 3 stars (100% destruction), 0.2 POL is added to spoil
  • For each high-level defensive building (10+) destroyed, 5% additional resources are received
  • For each consecutive successful attack, 10% additional resources are received (maximum 50%)
  • Maximum lootable resources: 150,000 gold, 100,000 wood, 50 diamonds, 10 elixir

B) Destruction Attack

  • Base War Score: (Destruction percentage × 10) + (number of buildings destroyed × 5)
  • Opponent Level Coefficient: 1 + (opponent tribe level ÷ 10)
  • League Coefficient:
    • Bronze: 1
    • Silver: 1.2
    • Gold: 1.5
    • Diamond: 2
  • Final Score: Base war score × opponent level coefficient × league coefficient

Additional Rewards:

  • Special prize for 100% destruction = 3 additional points + 0.5 POL
  • Chain bonus: 5 additional points for each consecutive successful attack
  • In case of defeat = minimum 10% of base score (for effort)

C) Rival Tribe Attack (Token Theft)

  • Base Capturable Token Amount: (5% of opponent tribe tokens in treasury) × (destruction percentage ÷ 100)
  • Protection Coefficient: 1 - (opponent treasury level × 0.05) (minimum 0.5)
  • Alliance Coefficient:
    • If opponent tribe is alliance member: 0.8
    • Otherwise: 1
  • Capturable Token: Base token amount × protection coefficient × alliance coefficient

Limitations:

  • Maximum capturable token in each attack: 1% of total tribe token supply
  • 5% chance of receiving a random low-level NFT in case of complete victory
  • For every 3 consecutive successful attacks, 1% chance of receiving a rare NFT
